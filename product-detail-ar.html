About

Argon is one of the most widely used members of the inert gas family, accounting for approximately 0.934% of the Earth's atmosphere. With its outer electron shell full, it reacts almost never at room temperature and pressure.

Argon is commonly used in welding protection, gas-protected lighting, food and beverage argon filling, semiconductor manufacturing protection gas, and inert atmosphere control in many industrial scenarios.
